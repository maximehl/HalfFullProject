What is depression?

Depression is not just feeling sad--it can make you feel hopeless, unmotivated, or worthless, or gain or lose weight rapidly. Depression is linked to insomnia or excessive sleeping, lack of energy, trouble concentrating, and suicidal thoughts or a desire to self-harm. It's one of the most common mental disorders, and affects many people our age, but it is treatable.

Many people with depression find a balance of therapy and antidepressant medication, although everyone's different and some things don't work for everyone in the same way. What's important is to keep trying.

While it's hard to self-diagnose, since everyone expresses symptoms differently and you might not show all of the symptoms, having a baseline can be helpful. If you're worried you've got depression, you can progress through this simple test to find out how urgent it is that you get help.

Please realize that we are not professionals! We got all of this information from the resources we listed for you and our personal experiences, and while we made sure it's accurate, you are the best judge of yourself! If you're worried about your mental health or feel like you're in a dangerous place, you should consider seeking out someone who is qualified to help you. Depression is a disease like any other, and a qualified professional is the best place to go to get help for it. Start with the resources above and make sure to do your own research!
